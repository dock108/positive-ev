The Math Behind the Magic

Kelly Criterion - The Optimal Betting Formula

The mathematical sweet spot: $$K = \frac{p(b-1) - (1-p)}{b-1}$$ where $p$ is win probability and $b$ is decimal odds. We use Quarter Kelly (0.25K) because we respect variance and want to sleep at night. Fun fact: this formula was originally used to optimize data transmission over noisy phone lines! üìû

Market Implied Probability - Reading the Market's Mind

Converting odds to probabilities using some slick math:
For positive odds: $$P_{implied} = \frac{100}{odds + 100}$$
For negative odds: $$P_{implied} = \frac{|odds|}{|odds| + 100}$$
When these differ from our models, that's where we strike.

The Composite Score Formula

Our proprietary scoring system combines multiple normalized metrics (0-100 scale):

$$Score = 0.60 \times EV_{score} + 0.30 \times Edge_{score} + 0.05 \times Time_{score} + 0.05 \times Kelly_{score}$$

  • $EV_{score}$: Normalized EV% on 0-100 scale $$EV_{score} = (EV\% + 10) \times 5$$
  • $Edge_{score}$: Normalized edge on 0-100 scale $$Edge_{score} = (Edge\% + 10) \times 5$$
  • $Time_{score}$: 100 for >24h, linear decrease to 0 at event time
  • $Kelly_{score}$: Normalized Kelly criterion $$Kelly_{score} = (Kelly + 0.1) \times 500$$
